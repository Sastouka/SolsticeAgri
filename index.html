Solstice Agri

Privacy Policy & Data Safety

Last Updated: January 16, 2026

Sastouka Digital develops the Solstice Agri mobile application. This policy details how data is handled to ensure your privacy and compliance with Google Play Store regulations.

1. "Offline-First" Architecture

Solstice Agri is designed to operate primarily offline. This means that:

  • All data you input is stored locally on your device within a secure database (Hive).
  • We do not operate a central server that collects, stores, or analyzes your agricultural data.
  • No data is automatically transferred to Sastouka Digital.

2. Collected Data & Purpose

The application processes the following data categories to ensure its functionality (profitability calculations, production tracking):

Data Type Detail (Examples) Purpose
Prof. Identity Name, Role (Engineer/Technician) Interface customization and PDF report signing.
Agri Data Harvest weights, varieties, plots, fertilizer doses, irrigation Dashboard generation, stock tracking, and traceability.
Financial Data Labor costs, selling prices, expenses (invoices) Calculation of Net Margin and profitability indicators (Engineer Module).
Files Images (Logo), Generated PDFs Report customization and data export.

3. Required Permissions

To provide its services, the application may request the following permissions:

Storage (Read/Write)

This permission is essential to:

  • Generate and save PDF reports ("Activity Report").
  • Export data to Excel or JSON (Backup).
  • Restore a backup from the phone's internal storage.

Camera / Gallery (Optional)

Used only if you wish to import an image (farm logo) to appear in the header of your PDF reports.

4. Third-Party Services

Although the app is offline, it interacts with certain system services:

  • Google Play Billing: Used to manage Premium subscriptions (Monthly, Yearly, Lifetime). Google processes financial transactions; we never see your banking details.
  • System Share: When you click "Share PDF", the app passes the file to the app of your choice (WhatsApp, Gmail, Drive).

5. Data Deletion (Right to be Forgotten)

Since data is stored locally, you have total and immediate control:

Option A: Deletion via App

Go to Settings > Danger Zone > Clear Data. This action irreversibly erases the entire local database (Hive).

Option B: Uninstall

Uninstalling the application automatically deletes all data stored in the application's private folder on your device.
